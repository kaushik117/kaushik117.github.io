About

Architecting enterprise-grade financial platforms

My recent work has centered on designing a multi-tenant lending platform covering Loan Management System (LMS), collections, centralized IAM, platform integration, and cloud engineering foundations. I have designed the LMS and Collections platform from scratch with modular microservices, strong tenant isolation, configurable behavior, secure integration patterns, and scalable deployment foundations.

A major part of my work has been building the complete IAM and authorization architecture using Keycloak, including tenant-aware realm strategy, authentication flows, token handling, RBAC/ABAC-aligned access control, and secure microservice integration.

I also work closely with engineering teams on design governance, code and architecture review, performance and scalability considerations, DevOps setup, CI/CD pipeline standardization, and reusable platform patterns. More recently, I have been exploring Agentic AI, MCP-based integrations, guardrails, and AI-enabled architecture patterns for enterprise financial services.

Core focus: Translate complex business problems into robust, secure, configurable platforms that are built for scale.
